Our Story

From Vision to Impact

Youth for Children (YFC) was founded in 2023 by Stephen Okoth and Joy Tadayo, who began as teaching assistants through Pacemaker International at Magadi Primary School. As part of their fellowship, they designed a capstone project addressing inequalities that hinder children from accessing quality education.

Through close interactions with learners, they discovered that many were facing different forms of abuse. This inspired the creation of a Child Protection and Safeguarding curriculum (CPAS), designed to empower children to know themselves, use their voices, and advocate for their rights.

The program successfully impacted 88 learners (40 boys and 46 girls aged 10â€“12). However, this also revealed the widespread prevalence of abuse and the urgent need for sustainable action. In response, YFC was born with a unique approach: empowering youth with the skills to directly empower children.

YFC believes that young people, having experienced and overcome systemic challenges, are well-placed to cushion the pathway for children and ensure safer, more inclusive learning environments. Since then, YFC has expanded its reach across Kisumu, Nairobi, Machakos, and Kilifi, reaching 255 children in 2025.

Our Foundation

Our Vision

A just and flourishing society where children are empowered to be agents of their own meaningful change, inspired by the leadership of youth.

Our Mission

To build a just and flourishing society through youth-led programs that strengthen inclusive education and empower children to respond to contemporary issues, including child abuse and climate change.

Our Approach

YFC believes in collective impact and works closely with teachers, schools, and fellows from organizations such as Teach for Kenya and Pacemaker International to create sustainable change.
